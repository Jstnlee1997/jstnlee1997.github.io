Government Techonology Agency

Software Engineering Intern ~ June 2020 - September 2020

I was mainly involved in two projects in GovTech.
In my first project, I successfully developed a Decision Weighted Tree for a 3D Car Park Algorithm to aid drivers with different variables (such as distance from the lift lobby) in the decision making of which is their optimum parking lot that ... meets the minimum criteria weightage. The algorithm would then determine the shortest route to the destination using a depth-first search method. It was a particularly challenging project as I had minimal supervision and I had to code in JavaScript, a language I was not familiar with then. However, I thoroughly enjoyed the process of having to simulataneously research on existing studies, learn graph theory and various algorithms, and also how to code in JavaScript.

The second project requried me to work in a team to design a Static Map of Singapore and its neighbours. I was responsible for stitching over 4000 satellite images into larger texture tiles, and I utilised Python to automate the otherwise tedious process. After which, I had to self-learn how to use Photoshop and Autodesk 3ds Max to create textures and UV unwrap them on existing 3D meshes to make the map and individual buildings look more refined and aesthetically pleasing to the viewer.

PROJECTS

Several ways of how I have implemented my skills in order to hone my craft

  • Embedded C Microcontroller


    My partner and I had to design, make, and test a functional mechatronic system - a buggy, to navigate a "mine" using a series of instructions coded in coloured cards. Once it has located its objective, or has gone astray for too long, it would then return to its original position.

    We also had to ensure that the buggy was functional in any light conditions and terrain, which means that we had to develop calibration techniques that were simple enough to understand and carry out by anyone.

  • Twitter-like App

    Developed this app using Ruby on Rails that employs a Model-View-Controller (MVC) architectural pattern. I purchased a guide from Learn Enough to prepare myself for my internship with Talenox.

    It was my first time coding a website application from scratch which has only fueled my passion for web app development.

  • Carpark Algorithm

    This was the output of the carpark algorithm that I had discussed earlier during my internship experience with GovTech. The algorithm helps the users to visualize the carpark using a matrix, where they can easily create parking lots with different conditions suited to the drivers' needs. The algo can also output the exact routes that the drivers would take to reach their optimum lot.

  • Simulation of Diffusion of Water Pollution

    Utilised mathematical numerical methods to create a 2D simulation of how a drop of oil would diffuse in a river with respect to time. The objective was to essentially use code to resolve complex differential equations where the parameters vary in space and time.

    The concentration of the oil was skewed due to uneven conditions in the river (cross-current). It was particularly challenging to obtain stable simulations for big and fine grids.
